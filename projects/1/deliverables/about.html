My Story

I sat down in, “Intro to Social Entrepreneurship” in Boulder in 2012 thinking I would learn how to become the next Mark Zuckerberg. Well, the first assignment of the class was, “What is social entrepreneurship?” and I was way off. We learned about the developing world and BFPs like global poverty, environmental destruction, and humanitarian crises. We learned that many have given up hope, and that these challenges have been deemed impossible to overcome. I was pleasantly surprised to learn that this class would teach me how to 1) identify an opportunity, in this case a social, environmental, and economic challenge, and 2) how to use business as a tool to overcome that challenge. Most importantly, 3) my professor, Francy Milner, inspired a hope that transformed into a burning desire. The class not only taught me how to think like an entrepreneur, it changed how I defined success, with 3 bottom lines and generations-to-come, in mind.
